# Secure Data Destruction Policy

Holoul provides certified destruction of data-bearing media for customers with
strict confidentiality and regulatory requirements (banking, healthcare,
government, and telecom).

## Accepted data-bearing media

Hard disk drives (HDD), solid-state drives (SSD), magnetic tapes and cartridges,
flash memory, optical media (DVD/CD), and mobile phones.

## Destruction methods

- **Degaussing** — exposes magnetic media to a strong magnetic field, rendering
  data unrecoverable. Used for HDDs and tapes.
- **Physical Destruction** — mechanical crushing or drilling of drives.
- **Shredding** — media is shredded into fragments that cannot be reconstructed.
  Suitable for the highest security requirements and for SSDs.

## Chain of custody

Every data destruction job is assigned a unique **certificate number**. The
destruction event is recorded on CCTV. Customers may request video verification.
The certificate records the number of devices destroyed, the method used, and the
date, providing an auditable chain of custody.

## On-site vs in-facility

Destruction can be performed on the customer's premises using Holoul's mobile
service, or at a Holoul facility after secure collection. On-site destruction is
recommended where media must never leave the customer's control before being
rendered unreadable.
